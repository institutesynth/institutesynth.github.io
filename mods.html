Mod #1: Judy Alvarez Outfit

This was my first mod for Fallout 4. The outfit is worn by Judy Alvarez in Cyberpunk 2077. Because Cyberpunk 2077 is a popular game, there are a lot of 3D models of her online. I downloaded a 3D model of her from here since the creator is known to make high quality models and organises the pieces and textures for the model very well. After, I had to match the model to Fallout 4's female body using 3DSMax. After that, another round of matching the outfit to the body occurs in Outfit Studio, as well as the dreaded bone weighting. I have revisited this mod after making it since the first product was not the best. Modding is difficult, but for some reason, it's more fun than playing the actual game.

When I first made this mod, I would join the outfit pieces to their respective piece. For example, I would have joined the stitching on the overall to the actual overall set. This was an issue because (1) the stitching was its own separate texture set, and (2) when you combine, you could only map one texture set to the piece, and (3) the overalls lose their detailing. To be frank, I think modding is analogous to medicine, where you need to work with what you have and plan accordingly. You don't really know how someone will split their textures, and you have to do a quick study on the files before you go about rigging it for Fallout 4.

Mod #2: Connor RK800 Outfit

And we're off to the second mod, which is Connor's RK800 outfit from Detroit: Become Human. For a little bit of background, the game follows the story of 3 Androids, or human-like robot servants. A company based in Detroit, called Cyberlife, designs and programs all these robots. One of the Android models they make is the RK800 model, commonly named Connor. His model is used to assist in police investigations (the other Androids you play as are mainly household models). Once again, I used a 3D model and adapted it to Fallout 4 standards. A difficulty I had with this is that it was a full outfit. Tank tops and skimpy outfits are usually easier since you don't need to worry about the sleeves aligning with the arms. However, Connor's arms were longer than the Fallout 4 model, so I needed to hem the jacket. Moreover, I had to rig the 3D model's body in XNALara (posing software) to make it match the Fallout 4 body, as it was originally in a T-pose, and the Fallout 4 body is in an A-pose. Another difficulty is that Connor is a slim guy, but the default Fallout 4 male body is much bigger and muscular, so it was a little difficult increasing mesh volume. I learned a lot more about 3D modelling doing this outfit. It's not in the best state, and I'm planning to revisit it and optimise it more in the future. Also speaking of Detroit: Become Human, I think the morality explored by the game is becoming more relevant as we see more advances in AI technology.

Mod #3: Adidas Mech Arm

This mod is slightly different from my previous ones, as it's a mashup of shared assets and not a port from another game. There is this popular mechanical arm mod, but it is very difficult to actually equip in the game. However, I saw that people still managed to use it in their outfits. With several hours of experience doing those previous mods, I figured out how to easily add the arm to any outfit in Outfit Studio. I strung together some pieces in Outfit Studio, and then added the mech arm. I made this Adidas-themed, since I used to always wear their shoes for skateboarding (Nora's pro models are the best) and the 3 stripes are appealing, whether in the present-day, some cyberpunk dystopian future, or a post-apocalyptic wasteland.
